⬛︎ Purpose



The primary purpose of Shooting Dreams was to create a poetic and emotionally immersive space where users could externalise and release their inner thoughts or dreams. Rather than offering conventional feedback mechanisms or task-oriented goals, the project aimed to evoke a quiet sense of catharsis and reflection through symbolic visual transitions. By allowing the user to write a dream, choose an emotion, and then “send” it into the sky, the experience simulates a form of gentle emotional release, akin to whispering a wish to the stars.

Importantly, dreams are often constructed from personal emotional responses to current life situations. As such, the act of writing a dream becomes an opportunity for self-reflection, allowing users to pause and observe their own emotional states. The project thus becomes not just a space for expression, but a mirror for interiority-inviting users to quietly examine what lies beneath their subconscious surface.

⬛︎ Worth



Beyond novelty, Shooting Dreams prioritised emotional authenticity, gentle introspection, and poetic immersion as core values. The project did not seek to impress through technological complexity or visual spectacle, but rather through a quiet sensibility-inviting users to experience subtle emotional release and personal reflection.

It valued emotional presence over performance-based feedback, trusting that the simple act of expressing a dream held intrinsic worth, even without external validation. It trusted that users would bring their own meaning to the process, and that the act of expressing a dream was intrinsically valuable, even if no explicit feedback was given in return.

⬛︎ Framing



The design of Shooting Dreams was shaped by both technical and conceptual constraints that helped clarify its expressive goals. Technically, the project was built entirely with vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, avoiding external libraries to maintain simplicity and creative control. Initially, a Local Storage feature was considered to allow users to save and revisit their dreams. However, this functionality was ultimately removed-not because it clashed with the project’s emotional tone, but because it was deemed outside the intended scope of the assignment. As a result, the project's focus shifted from memory and retrieval to release and transience, transforming the act of writing a dream into a symbolic gesture of letting go, rather than preserving them.

On a conceptual level, the project rejected goal-driven logic or explicit interaction in favour of ambiguity, slowness, and emotional atmosphere. The user journey was structured as a light emotional ritual: choosing a feeling, writing a dream, and watching it rise and dissolve into the sky. The absence of feedback or persistent data was intentional, ensuring that the experience remained ephemeral and reflective. These self-imposed limitations reinforced the project’s central aim-to offer a poetic space where inner emotions could be expressed and released, rather than stored or judged.

⬛︎ Appearance



The visual language of Shooting Dreams was carefully composed to evoke emotional resonance through simplicity, symbolism, and space. The project avoided detailed illustrations or dense visuals, instead embracing a minimal aesthetic that supports introspection. A key design decision was the use of emotion-based color coding: each selected feeling triggered a background or star color that subtly reflected the user's emotional state. This chromatic symbolism offered users a nonverbal but intuitive way to feel seen and acknowledged.

The background remained mostly dark and subdued, invoking a night sky atmosphere and allowing text, stars, and transitions to emerge softly from the void. Transitions were slow and smooth, using opacity and fade techniques rather than cuts or abrupt changes, in order to preserve a sense of calm continuity. Instead of using literal metaphors or narrative visuals, the project leaned into symbolic abstraction, letting the user’s imagination complete the picture. This restraint fostered a contemplative visual space where emotion could unfold slowly and uninterrupted.

⬛︎ Quality


The technical implementation of Shooting Dreams was shaped by a desire to create a lightweight yet emotionally immersive experience. All components were built using HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript, with no external libraries or frameworks. This decision was not only a practical one-ensuring broad browser compatibility and fast loading-but also an aesthetic one, preserving the simplicity and softness central to the project’s tone.

Rather than focusing on functional complexity, the project emphasised timing, rhythm, and atmosphere. Key interactions-such as page transitions, dialog timing, emotion-based star changes, and animated text-were scripted manually using JavaScript and DOM manipulation. Visual effects like twinkling stars, floating headings, and fade-in messages were implemented through custom CSS keyframe animations, allowing for precise control over pacing and visual texture.

This technical restraint helped maintain emotional focus. The minimalist implementation kept the interface quiet and unobtrusive, encouraging viewers to concentrate on mood and meaning rather than on interface mechanics. Every technical decision-down to the timing of animations and the layering of sound-was made to support the feeling of sending a dream into space, slowly and sincerely.

⬛︎ Composition



The overall composition of Shooting Dreams was designed to create a unified emotional arc across three sequential visual stages. Each page-main, writing, and sending-is arranged to follow a slow and intentional rhythm, allowing the user to gradually move from passive observation to emotional release.

Visual layers were arranged with care to guide user attention without distraction. Static full-screen background images provide immersive continuity, while floating animations and dialog boxes emerge at carefully timed intervals. These visual moments were paced not for information delivery but for emotional build-up, encouraging stillness and reflection between actions.

Audio and visual transitions were also used to unify the experience. Looping background music provides consistency throughout the interaction, while the final page uses a fade-in message and animated star to gently close the interaction. Together, these elements form a rhythm of entry, expression, and release-composing the project as a meditative cycle rather than a functional tool.

⬛︎ Navigation



The navigation of Shooting Dreams was intentionally designed to follow a simple, linear flow with minimal user control. Rather than allowing users to click freely between pages, interaction is guided step-by-step-starting from the main page, progressing through a timed dream-writing sequence, and ending with a symbolic sending screen. This deliberate linearity reinforces the feeling of a ritual or journey.

Each interaction was paced carefully. For example, after clicking the heading, the second page does not immediately display the input dialog. Instead, the character appears, lingers, and only then does the dialog open-encouraging a reflective pause. By structuring navigation through time-based progression and visual transitions, the project encourages the user to surrender control and follow the emotional flow rather than navigate actively.

Non-verbal cues such as animations, fade-ins, and ambient sound further guide the user without requiring instruction. Navigation was not about moving through content efficiently, but about choreographing an experience of emotional pacing and eventual release.
