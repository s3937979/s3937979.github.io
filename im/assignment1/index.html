1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

When I accessed the website, the first thing that caught my eye was the effect of resizing the browser window. The screen initially displayed a beach at dawn in warm reddish hues, with a small airplane parked by the dock. However, as I narrowed the window, the sun began to rise slowly over the horizon, and the entire color palette of the scene transformed. The red tones of early dawn gradually faded, giving way to blue tones of morning. It felt as though I was shifting the time of day through my interaction, and this subtle transition creates a sense of connection between myself and the experience.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

During my interaction, I resized the browser window to observe how the visual scene would respond. I checked whether there was any background music, and then focused on how the screen transitioned while dragging the wheel at the bottom of the screen, I paused occasionally to watch the background animations and read the accompanying text. I also clicked on images to see what kinds of animation effects would be triggered through interaction.

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time on reading the poem in three chapters, The Archipelago, The Beach, and The City, with an animated background of the experience. The illustration movement was visually engaging, which allowed me to concentrate on the words while still feeling immersed in the atmosphere. It felt like the visuals and the text were part of a single artistic flow, encouraging me to linger longer in that moment.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action I repeated during the two minute interaction was dragging the wheel at the bottom of the screen with my mouse. Each drag triggered a transition to the next scene, so I kept interacting with it to explore how the different scenes were structured and connected.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The primary goal of this interactive experience is to promote the limited edition watch through a sensorial and artistic journey. Instead of directly presenting product specifications, the website immerses the user in a three-chapter narrative built around the theme of an “Endless Summer”. This experience encourages users to emotionally connect with the product, helping them understand its essence not through technical features, but through mood, visual atmosphere, and storytelling.

← →

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The experience communicates its goal without providing any direct information about the watch. It uses animated backgrounds combined with poetic texts and cozy background music to create a warm, immersive atmosphere. Additionally, the drag wheel at the bottom of the screen lets users move through scenes as if they are moving through time. The structure reinforces the feeling that the watch is not just a product, but part of a flowing, emotional journey.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?
(For how long and how many different times)

The website seems designed for a short, focused interaction of roughly five minutes in one sitting. With its three chapters and continuous visual flow, it feels like an art film that is meant to be experienced all at once.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

It communicates that the experience should be shown in a linear, time-based flow. The timeline is described on the bottom wheel, which marks the passage of time as users progress through the chapters like morning, afternoon, night in a day. However, the presence of a button that allows users to re-read the poem suggests that some elements are meant to be revisited. The experience balances a linear, immersive structure with small interactive moments that invite reflection and repeated engagement, especially with the more poetic aspects.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

I believe the website is like an art film that centers on the passage of the time and unfolds with a strong emotional rhythm, making it feel more like a visual piece than a conventional website. At the same time, it reminded me of luxury watch campaign sites, where the goal is not to present product specs but to immerse the user in a mood. It also felt similar to a visual interactive essay, blending animation, poetry, and user interaction in a way that made it feel like a digital poetry book.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

The references suggest that I should not rush through the experience and be navigated for information. Instead, it invites a slower, more mindful way of engaging like watching an art film or reading a poetry book. It is not about finding answers or checking boxes, but about feeling the mood, taking in the visuals, and letting the experience unfold at its own pace. I felt encouraged to respond emotionally, not analytically.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The experience encourages a calm, reflective emotional state. The colors, the poetic writing, the soft transitions, and the cozy background music all work together to create a warm, soothing atmosphere. Instead of making me feel rushed or analytical, it invited me to slow down and simply feel. It felt like being suspended in a slow summer afternoon.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part of the interaction was that the experience only worked properly when the browser window was resized to a narrow width or when viewed on a mobile device. On a regular desktop screen, key visuals did not display as intended unless I manually adjusted the window size. I felt like the full effect was only accessible under specific conditions, which could be frustrating for users on different devices or screen setups.

← →

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part of the experience was the way to drag the wheel around the bottom of the screen to move between scenes. Unlike a simple click or scroll, it felt like I was physically shifting time with my own hand, which made the interaction feel fresh and personal. The transitions were smooth and visually cohesive, allowing for emotional immersion while also giving me a sense of control over the flow of the experience.
